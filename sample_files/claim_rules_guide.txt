CLAIM PROCESSING RULES AND GUIDELINES

1. ELIGIBILITY
Claims are only accepted from active policyholders with a valid policy at the time of the incident. The incident must fall within the coverage period.

2. CLAIM TYPES AND LIMITS
- Medical Claims: Covered up to the policy coverage amount minus deductible. Requires hospital bills, doctor reports, and prescription receipts.
- Auto/Collision Claims: Requires police report, photos of damage, and repair estimates. Coverage up to insured vehicle value.
- Property Claims: Requires photos, contractor repair estimates, and proof of ownership. Covered up to property coverage limit.
- Life Insurance Claims: Requires certified death certificate, beneficiary identification, and completed claim form.

3. DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED
All claims must include:
- Completed claim form
- Proof of identity (government-issued ID)
- Incident report or supporting evidence
- Receipts or invoices for expenses claimed

4. PROCESSING TIMELINE
- Acknowledgement: Within 24 hours of submission
- Initial Review: 3-5 business days
- Investigation (if required): Up to 15 business days
- Final Decision: Within 30 days of complete documentation

5. CLAIM APPROVAL CRITERIA
Claims are approved when:
- The incident is covered under the policy
- All required documentation is provided
- No fraud or misrepresentation is detected
- The claim amount does not exceed the coverage limit

6. CLAIM REJECTION REASONS
- Incident occurred outside coverage period
- Claim type is excluded under the policy
- Insufficient or fraudulent documentation
- Claim filed after the 90-day deadline
- Pre-existing condition exclusion applies

7. APPEALS PROCESS
Rejected claims may be appealed within 30 days of the rejection notice. Submit a written appeal with additional supporting documentation to the claims review board.
